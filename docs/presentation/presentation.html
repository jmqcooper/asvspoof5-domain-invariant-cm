Domain-Adversarial Training for
Codec-Robust Speech Deepfake Detection

MSc Artificial Intelligence Thesis

Mike Cooper
University of Amsterdam · February 2026

Research Question

Do speech deepfake detectors use codec information as a shortcut, and if so, can DANN mitigate shortcut learning?

Sub-questions

  1. RQ1: Does DANN reduce the out-of-domain (OOD) gap?
  2. RQ2: Does DANN improve per-codec performance?
  3. RQ3: Where is domain information removed?
  4. RQ4: What components cause domain invariance?

The Problem: Codec Mismatch

Training Data

  • Clean, uncompressed audio
  • No codec diversity
  • Lab conditions

Real World

  • Compressed (MP3, AAC, Opus)
  • Voice calls (AMR, Speex)
  • Neural codecs (Encodec)
ASVspoof 5 Dataset
SplitCodec Diversity
Train0% (all uncoded)
Dev0% (all uncoded)
Eval75% coded (11 codecs)
Key challenge: Models trained on clean audio face unseen codec conditions at evaluation time.

ASVspoof5 §4.2 identifies five shortcut artifacts in the data (peak amplitude, energy, duration, leading/trailing silence) and applies post-processing to reduce them in dev/eval. Separately, §4.1 notes codec variation is applied only to the eval set. This creates a domain shift that detectors must generalize across.

Synthetic Codec Augmentation

Why?

Train/dev have no codec diversity — DANN needs domain labels to learn from.

How?

Apply synthetic codecs via ffmpeg during training:

  • MP3 (64-320 kbps)
  • AAC (32-256 kbps)
Training Distribution
  • 50% original (uncoded)
  • 25% MP3
  • 25% AAC
Key insight: Even with only 2 synthetic codecs, DANN achieves significant improvement on unseen codecs
Important: Training uses synthetic codec labels (MP3/AAC via FFmpeg). Evaluation uses real ASVspoof5 codec IDs (C01-C11). This is transfer of learned invariance to unseen real-world codecs — not direct supervised domain adaptation.

WavLM: DANN improves eval EER by 1.13 percentage points (8.47% → 7.34%) and minDCF by 0.054 (0.639 → 0.585). Trade-off: slightly higher dev EER (3.26% → 4.76%) for significantly better generalization.
W2V2: DANN improves eval EER by 0.97 pp (15.30% → 14.33%).

W2V2 minDCF saturates at 1.0 due to over-confident score distributions.

RQ1: Does DANN Reduce the OOD Gap?

✓ Yes. WavLM: Dev-to-Eval degradation drops from 5.21 pp (ERM) to 2.58 pp (DANN).
Figure: OOD gap (Eval EER − Dev EER) for ERM vs DANN
Interpreting OOD gaps across models: SSL models (WavLM, W2V2) learn rich representations that can latch onto domain-specific cues — high capacity leads to large OOD gaps, which DANN reduces. TRILLsson's small OOD gap reflects the opposite: its paralinguistic features are too generic to overfit to codec-specific artifacts, so it generalizes consistently — but at a much higher error rate. LFCC-GMM has a very large gap because handcrafted features are brittle under any distribution shift.

RQ4: Where Does Invariance Come From?

✓ Pooling weights + projection head — not the frozen backbone.
Learned layer pooling weights (softmax-normalized). DANN redistributes attention from early layers (L0–L2) to middle layers (L3–L5) where task-relevant features dominate over codec artifacts.
  • Backbone is frozen — hidden states are identical between ERM and DANN
  • All invariance arises from learnable pooling weights + downstream projection
  • ERM favours lower layers (L0 = 12.7%); DANN shifts to mid-layers (L4 = 11.1%)

RQ4: Weighted Contribution Similarity (CKA)

CKA computed on weighted layer contributions (hidden state × pooling weight), not raw hidden states. Raw hidden states have CKA ≈ 1.000 since the backbone is frozen.
Weighted contribution CKA per layer — upper layers diverge most
Ablation: component patching

CKA computed on WavLM representations. Since the backbone is frozen, layers 0-11 produce identical hidden states for both ERM and DANN. The low CKA at layer 11 reflects the learned mixing weights (which determine how much each layer contributes to the final representation) — DANN learns to weight layer 11 very differently from ERM, even though the layer's own features are identical.

Key Ablation: Augmentation ≠ DANN

Does codec augmentation alone improve OOD generalization, or is the adversarial objective necessary?
Model Backbone Eval EER Δ vs ERM
ERM WavLM 8.47% —
ERM+Aug WavLM 7.98% −0.49 pp
DANN WavLM 7.34% −1.13 pp
ERM W2V2 15.30% —
ERM+Aug W2V2 18.02% +2.72 pp
DANN W2V2 14.33% −0.97 pp
Conclusion: For WavLM, augmentation alone helps (−0.49 pp) but DANN provides additional gains beyond augmentation (−1.13 pp) — augmentation alone is insufficient to match DANN. For W2V2, augmentation hurts (+2.72 pp) while DANN still improves (−0.97 pp), showing the adversarial objective provides robustness that simple augmentation cannot.

Bounding Confounds: Shortcut Classifier

Are shortcuts (duration, energy, silence) explaining the OOD gap?

Method: Train logistic regression on 5 continuous-valued shortcut features only (no audio content). If it achieves high accuracy on train but fails on dev/eval → shortcuts are train-specific artifacts, not the cause of the OOD gap.

Split Accuracy AUC EER
Train 82.6% 0.903 17.7%
Dev 24.5% 0.451 53.6%
Eval 20.4% 0.483 52.7%

5 features from ASVspoof5 §4.2:
peak amplitude, energy, duration, leading/trailing silence

✓ Shortcuts are TRAIN-SPECIFIC
High train accuracy (82.6%) but chance-level on dev/eval (~50% EER)
Conclusion: OOD gap is NOT explained by these five shortcut artifacts (ASVspoof5 §4.2). This is consistent with codec shift being a major driver of the performance gap; other distribution differences may still contribute.

Features are used as raw continuous values (e.g., exact duration in seconds, energy level) rather than binarized (present/absent). This gives the classifier maximum information — and it still fails on dev/eval, strengthening the conclusion.

Novelty & Contributions

"Systematic evaluation of DANN for codec-robust deepfake detection, including transfer from synthetic to real codecs and representation-level analysis."
  • DANN applied specifically to codec mismatch problem
  • Frozen backbone + DANN = invariance in projection layer only
  • Probing + CKA analysis provides interpretability
  • Ablation confirms augmentation ≠ adversarial training
Practical takeaway: Domain invariance doesn't require fine-tuning billion-parameter models — a lightweight projection head is sufficient

Limitations

  • 2 synthetic codecs (MP3, AAC)
    OPUS failed silently during training
  • Backbone matters more
    WavLM (7.34%) >> W2V2 (14.33%)
  • Frozen backbone
    Limits where invariance can emerge
  • No MMD/CORAL comparison
    Other DA methods not evaluated
  • Single training seed
    Bootstrap CIs on eval only
  • W2V2 minDCF saturation
    Scores near 1.0 → minDCF uninformative
  • Not mechanistic interp
    We know where, not how

Future Work

  • Partial backbone unfreezing
    Unfreezing the top 2-3 transformer layers would allow DANN's adversarial gradient to reshape backbone representations directly. CKA analysis shows layer 11 already diverges significantly in mixing weights alone — even partial unfreezing could yield substantial further invariance gains.
  • More codec diversity
    Neural codecs (Encodec), device/channel effects
  • Compare with MMD, CORAL
    Other domain adaptation methods
  • Multi-seed experiments
    Capture training variance
